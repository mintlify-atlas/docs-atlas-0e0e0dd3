## Legal Framework Covered

The legal information section covers key Mexican regulations applicable to health websites:

### 1. Health and Advertising Regulations (COFEPRIS)

<Accordion title="Ley General de Salud (LGS)">
  **Article 300**: Advertising must not attribute preventive, therapeutic, or rehabilitative qualities to products or services that don't have them.
  
  **Article 310**: Advertising of health services requires authorization or notice from the Ministry of Health through COFEPRIS.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Advertising Regulation (RLGSMP)">
  **Article 8**: Requires professional credentials (cédula profesional) to be displayed visibly.
  
  **Article 79**: Prohibits:
  - Promising infallible results
  - Using patient testimonials without specific permissions
  - Offering free consultations as promotional tools
  - Using ambiguous or overly technical language
  
  **Articles 118-119**: Details the process for obtaining advertising permits or filing notices with COFEPRIS.
</Accordion>

### 2. Data Protection (INAI)

<Accordion title="LFPDPPP - Personal Data Protection Law">
  **Article 3, Section VI**: Defines health data as sensitive personal data requiring highest protection.
  
  **Article 9**: Treatment of sensitive personal data requires explicit written consent.
  
  **Articles 15-16**: Requires a Privacy Notice (Aviso de Privacidad) to be accessible before users enter any data.
  
  **Article 19**: Mandates administrative, technical, and physical security measures (encryption, secure databases).
</Accordion>

### 3. Professional Practice Regulations

<Accordion title="Professional Licensing Requirements">
  **Article 25 (Ley de Profesiones)**: Requires professional credentials to be displayed prominently.
  
  **NOM-004-SSA3-2012**: Defines medical record structure and 5-year retention requirements.
  
  **NOM-024-SSA3-2012**: Regulates electronic health record systems (SIRES) for security and interoperability.
</Accordion>

### 4. Consumer Protection (PROFECO)

<Accordion title="LFPC - Consumer Protection Law">
  **Article 7**: All information must be truthful, verifiable, and clear.
  
  **Article 32**: Prohibits misleading advertising.
  
  **Article 76 Bis**: Regulates e-commerce transactions including:
  - Secure and confidential information handling
  - Clear pricing and service information
  - Accessible terms and conditions
</Accordion>

## Compliance Checklist

The legal information includes a practical checklist for compliance:

### Required Actions

1. **Legal Consultation**
   - Hire specialized legal counsel in health law and data protection

2. **COFEPRIS Registration**
   - Prepare website project documentation
   - File Advertising Notice or obtain Advertising Permit
   - Include professional credentials in filing
   - Do not launch until approval/acknowledgment received

3. **Legal Documents**
   - **Privacy Notice**: Comprehensive document for sensitive health data
   - **Terms and Conditions**: For appointment scheduling and payments
   - **Consent Mechanisms**: Explicit consent collection for data processing

4. **Website Design Requirements**
   - Display professional credentials (cédula profesional) prominently
   - Ensure all content is informative and scientifically accurate
   - Never promise guaranteed results
   - Implement mandatory consent checkboxes before form submission

5. **Technical Security**
   - SSL certificate (HTTPS)
   - Encrypted database storage
   - Access control measures
